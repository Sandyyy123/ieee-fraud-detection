1. Motivation

Card-not-present payment fraud is the dominant residual loss vector in European retail banking. Production fraud-detection systems share three constraints:

  • Cost-asymmetric errors. A missed fraud costs the chargeback amount; a blocked legitimate transaction costs customer churn plus operational review. Pure ROC-AUC is insufficient; the deliverable evaluates PR-AUC and a cost-weighted score.
  • Sub-second latency. Authorisation decisions resolve in <250 ms. Tree ensembles fit; full GNN inference does not, so the GNN is positioned as a near-real-time scoring layer (post-authorisation review queue, daily-cached embeddings).
  • Heavy class imbalance. ~3.5% on IEEE-CIS, often <0.5% in DACH retail-banking AML monitoring. Resampling, class weighting, and focal loss are all evaluated.
Regulatory framing. Under the EU AI Act, fraud and credit scoring are high-risk systems requiring documentation, transparency, and human oversight. Under BaFin operational-risk schedules, every model decision must be reproducible from logged inputs.

3. Methods

3.1 Validation protocol

Chronological 5-fold CV. Random k-fold inflates validation ROC-AUC by 0.02-0.04 on this dataset because it leaks future card / merchant information into training. Each fold trains on the first k-th of the timeline and validates on the next k-th, mirroring the Kaggle public/private split.

3.2 Baseline feature blocks (LightGBM)

  • Time decomposition. tx_day, tx_hour, tx_dow from TransactionDT.
  • Time-since-last per card1. dt_since_card1 = TransactionDT.diff() after sort.
  • Rolling aggregations per card1. Cumulative count, sum, mean of TransactionAmt (Whitrow 2008, Bahnsen 2016).
  • Device-OS interaction. DeviceType x id_30.
  • Frequency encoding for high-cardinality columns; training-fold frequency map reused on validation to avoid leakage.
  • V-block missingness aggregate. Single integer v_n_missing.
  • log1p(TransactionAmt) for the long right tail.

Hyperparameters: num_leaves=255, learning_rate=0.03, min_child_samples=80, is_unbalance=True, up to 2000 boosting rounds with 100-round early stopping.

3.3 Advanced: heterogeneous GNN

Three node types: Card (card1 hash), Merchant (ProductCD x addr1 x P_emaildomain), Device (DeviceInfo x id_30 x id_31). Three edge types: (Card, transacted_at, Merchant) (supervised, target = isFraud), (Card, used_from, Device), (Merchant, seen_via, Device).

Architecture: heterogeneous GraphSAGE (Hamilton 2017), 2 message-passing layers, hidden 128, dropout 0.3. Edge head concatenates source-card embedding, destination-merchant embedding, and edge attributes [log_amt, dt_norm]. Loss: focal loss (gamma=2, alpha=0.25) on the supervised edge type, with 5x positive-edge oversampling at the batch level (PC-GNN style).

Alternative backbone: Heterogeneous Graph Transformer (Hu 2020), four attention heads, held in reserve.
Fallback: Three-seed CatBoost ensemble (seeds 42, 1337, 7), mean-blended; triggered if PyG is unavailable or GNN val AUC < 0.85 after 30 epochs.

6. Discussion

Imbalance handling at two levels

The pipeline applies imbalance handling at both the sampler level (5x positive edge sampling for the GNN, is_unbalance=True for LightGBM) and the loss level (focal loss for the GNN). Either alone is insufficient at this scale of imbalance.

Anonymised features and graph construction

Vesta does not expose merchant identity. The (ProductCD, addr1, P_emaildomain) proxy is coarse and conflates many distinct merchants, biasing the GNN toward signals at the (product-category, region, email-domain) level. Real-world DACH systems retain hashed merchant IDs and avoid this proxy.

Production constraints not exercised

  • Authorisation latency. Production GNNs pre-compute embeddings on a daily schedule and serve a cached MLP head; not benchmarked here.
  • Delayed labels. Real card-fraud labels arrive after one week to one month; IEEE-CIS labels are fully supervised (optimistic relative to production).
  • Audit and explainability. TreeSHAP over LightGBM is in-scope; GNN integrated gradients on edge attributes is Phase 2.

7. Conclusion

  • Two-stage stack: LightGBM baseline (engineered tabular features, chronological 5-fold CV, class-imbalance handling) plus heterogeneous GraphSAGE on a card-merchant-device tri-partite graph (focal loss, 5x positive oversampling).
  • CatBoost ensemble fallback for environments without PyTorch Geometric.
  • Cost-weighted evaluation grounded in DACH card-not-present economics, alongside ROC-AUC and PR-AUC.
  • Phase 1 deliverables: scaffolded code, EDA notebook, manuscript, and references. Phase 2: run scripts, fill in numerical results, ship deployment-ready embeddings.
